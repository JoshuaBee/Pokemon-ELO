Type ELO

Pokémon have 18 possible types, it is essentially like rock, paper, scissors but with 18 possible hand shapes.
Here are a few Pokémon and their types:

Fire

Ground

Psychic

Each type also has weaknesses, resistances and immunities against the other types.
For example:
Fire is weak to Water, meaning a Water attack will do double damage to a Fire Pokémon.
Dragon resists Grass, meaning a Grass attack will do half damage to a Dragon Pokémon.
Flying is immune to Ground, meaning a Ground attack will do no damage to a Flying Pokémon.

One way to competitively rank similar competitors is by using the ELO system. In its most famous incarnation it is used to rank chess players, but it can be used to rank anything. We are going to use it to rank Pokémon types. A simple explanation of how ELO works is that winning matches wins you points, and losing matches loses you points. The points you earn/forfeit depend on the skill difference of the opponents. I like to visualise it in 4 different examples:

  • If I am much better than my opponent and I win, I don't get many points.
  • If I am much worse than my opponent and I win, I get a lot of points.
  • If I am much better than my opponent and I lose, I lose a lot of points.
  • If I am much worse than my opponent and I lose, I don't lose many points.




Unfortunately, Pokémon is a bit more complicated than this. Pokémon can have 2 types, which is equivalent to playing rock, paper, scissors with both hands at the same time.
Here are a few Pokémon with multiple types:

Rock

Water

Ghost

Poison

Bug

Flying

This also means both types are factored into the weaknesses, resistances and immunities calculations.
For example:
Both Fire and Rock are weak to Water, meaning a Water attack will do quadruple damage to a Fire Rock Pokémon.
Both Dragon and Poison resist Grass, meaning a Grass attack will do quarter damage to a Dragon Poison Pokémon.
Fighting resists Dark, and Ghost is weak to Dark, meaning a Dark attack will do neutral damage to a Fighting Ghost Pokémon.
